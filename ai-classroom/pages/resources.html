Additional Help and Resources

Stuck on something, or want to explore further on your own? Here is where to look next.

New to AI? Start with the "What is AI?" and "History" pages in the menu above, then take the quiz to test what you have learned.

Recommended Videos

3Blue1Brown's neural network series and Crash Course AI , both completely free , are excellent video explainers for visual learners.

📖

Recommended Reading

"AI Superpowers" by Kai-Fu Lee and "Life 3.0" by Max Tegmark , two of the most accessible books on the topic , are great for curious students.

🏫

Free Online Courses

Khan Academy, Coursera's "AI For Everyone," and Google's "AI Essentials" , all free and beginner-friendly , are great places to start.

🛠️

Troubleshooting AI Tools

Getting strange answers from a chatbot? Try being more specific, breaking your question into smaller parts, or giving an example of what you want.

🗂️

Glossary of AI Terms

Confused by a term like "token," "parameter," or "fine-tuning"? Check the definitions throughout our lessons, or ask us on the Contact page.

For Teachers and Parents

Looking for guidance on how to introduce AI concepts at home or in the classroom? We have collected age-appropriate talking points, available on request.

AI Tools for Job Hunting

Older students can use AI to polish resumes, prepare for interviews, and research companies , a practical, real-world application beyond the classroom.

Community and Forums

Reddit's r/artificial and Hugging Face's community forums are friendly, active spaces for questions and discussion about AI.
