Chapter 2

The History of AI

AI has been built by brilliant minds over decades of discovery, setbacks, and breakthroughs.

💡
1950

Alan Turing asks "Can Machines Think?"

British mathematician Alan Turing published a landmark paper proposing the "Turing Test," a way to measure whether a machine could exhibit intelligent behaviour indistinguishable from a human.

🎓
1956

The Birth of "Artificial Intelligence"

John McCarthy coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" at the Dartmouth Conference, which gathered top scientists to discuss the possibility of machines that could learn and reason.

🥶
1970s–80s

The "AI Winters"

Progress stalled and funding dried up twice. Computers were not powerful enough, and AI systems could not handle real-world complexity. These periods were called "AI winters."

♟️
1997

Deep Blue Beats the World Chess Champion

IBM's Deep Blue defeated Garry Kasparov, the reigning world chess champion, a milestone proving computers could outperform humans in complex strategic thinking.

📸
2012

Deep Learning Revolution

A neural network called AlexNet shattered records in image recognition, kicking off the modern deep learning era. Suddenly AI could see, hear, and understand the world far better than before.

💬
2022–Today

The Age of Generative AI

ChatGPT, Claude, DALL·E, and other generative AI tools became mainstream, allowing anyone, including students, to write, code, create art, and solve problems with AI assistance.
