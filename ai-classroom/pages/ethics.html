Using AI Responsibly

AI is a powerful tool, and like all powerful tools, it comes with responsibilities.

🎓

Academic Integrity

Using AI to do your work and submitting it as your own is dishonest. Use AI to help you learn, not to replace your thinking. Always check your school's AI policy.

🔍

Fact-Check Everything

AI can "hallucinate," confidently stating false information. Always verify important facts from AI with trusted sources like books, academic papers, or reputable websites.

🔒

Privacy and Safety

Never share personal information, passwords, or sensitive details with AI tools. Be aware of what data apps collect, and get a parent or guardian's guidance if unsure.

⚖️

Bias and Fairness

AI learns from human-created data, which can contain biases. AI systems can reflect and even amplify those biases, something researchers are actively working to fix.

🤝

Giving Credit

If you use AI to help shape an idea or draft, be transparent about it when your school or workplace asks. Honesty about your process builds trust.

🌱

Environmental Impact

Training and running large AI models uses significant electricity and water. Being mindful of how often and why you use AI is part of using it responsibly.
